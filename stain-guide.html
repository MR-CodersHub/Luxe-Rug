Spill Emergency

Artisan Stain Rescue Tips

A spill on a luxury rug requires immediate, careful action. Discover step-by-step emergency blot routines recommended by our master weavers.

Red Wine Spills

High Risk Liquid Spill
1
Blot Immediately (Do NOT Scrub)

Press clean white cotton towels or plain white paper towels firmly onto the wine spill. Press vertically to extract liquid; scrubbing will cause the red pigments to lock deep into the wool cuticle or silk core.

2
Apply Cool Distilled Water

Dampen a clean towel with a small amount of cool distilled water and continue blotting from the outside of the stain toward the center. This dilutes the sugars and tannins without bleeding.

3
Dry and Elevate

Place a dry towel underneath the rug if possible to prevent bottom moisture buildup, then use a hairdryer on an absolute COOL setting or a fan to dry the patch rapidly.

Critical Red Warning

Never apply standard salt, white wine, or baking soda to wool or silk rugs. Salt sets wine dyes permanently, and baking soda's high alkalinity burns delicate protein fibers, causing severe color running.

Coffee & Tea

Medium Risk Tannic liquid
1
Press & Blot

Soak up as much hot coffee as possible using dry, absorbent white microfibers. Change towels frequently as they saturate to avoid redistributing the acid stain.

2
Neutralize with White Vinegar Mixture

Mix 1 teaspoon of white vinegar with 1 cup of cool distilled water. Dampen a white cloth and blot. The mild acid neutralizes tannic acids present in coffee and tea, preventing pile yellowing.

3
Flush & Blot Dry

Blot with fresh water to rinse vinegar trace, then press a stack of heavy white towels under a weight to pull remaining moisture out.

Critical Red Warning

Never use domestic oxygen cleansers or dishwashing soap containing bleach. These chemical oxidizers destroy wool fiber structures and result in permanent bleached yellow patches.

Concierge Recommendation

If the coffee contained cream, milk fat residue will attract mold if not completely flushed. Professional organic deep pool washing is recommended for heavy spills.

Pet Stains (Urine & Vomit)

Extreme Risk Acid / Alkaline Organic
1
Firm Extraction

Scrape solid matter immediately using a spoon, and blot wet urine using thick layers of white towels. Step on the towel stack to maximize liquid extraction from deep base warps.

2
Mild Vinegar Sponge

Apply 50% distilled water and 50% white vinegar solution. Sponge lightly over the urine region. The vinegar neutralizes alkaline ammonia gas buildup, halting structural pile bleaching.

3
Frictionless Blotting

Gently blot the solution using a clean cloth. Elevate the rug section using blocks or a fan to ensure absolute dry foundation, as damp cotton warps undergo quick dry-rot.

Critical Red Warning

Never apply standard store pet enzyme sprays directly onto delicate wool or silk. These products contain enzymes that digest protein fibers, literally eating away the wool piles and weakening backing knots.

Complete Odor Eradication

Dried pet urine converts into alkaline salts that absorb moisture from the air, continuously releasing smells and rotting backing threads. Professional submersive pool flushes are mandatory to completely extract urine salt crystals.

Ink & Dye Spills

Extreme Risk Chemical Colorant
1
Contain the Area

Do NOT touch or wet the ink immediately, as ink spreads rapidly when exposed to standard liquids. Place dry white towels tightly around the margins to contain bleeding.

2
Rubbing Alcohol blotting (Wool Only)

Lightly moisten a cotton swab with standard rubbing alcohol. Blot (never rub) the ink tip directly. The alcohol dissolves solvent-based ink without destroying wool. Do not do this on silk.

3
Repeated Blotting

Press dry cotton buds firmly to transfer ink colorant. Continue changing cotton swabs to avoid smearing ink onto clean adjacent piles.

Critical Red Warning

Never saturate an ink stain with water, soap, or acetone. Acetone melts synthetic backing materials and runs organic dye colors instantly, destroying antique Persian textiles.

Restoration Specialist Alert

Ink has high color fastness and spreads like a wave. For silk, antique, or valuable rugs, skip DIY steps and contact our workshop immediately to schedule urgent collection.

Mud & Clay Soil

Medium Risk Particulate Dirt
1
Let the Mud Dry Completely

Unlike other stains, trying to clean wet mud will smear wet silt deep into the rug pile, creating a larger dirty footprint. Allow the mud patch to dry completely into a crust.

2
Crust Breakdown & Gentle Vacuuming

Lightly tap the dry crust with a wooden spoon to crumble the mud. Gently vacuum up loose dirt using flat suction (no brushes, to avoid slicing wool shafts).

3
Blot Remaining residue

If any soil tint remains, sponge carefully with 1 cup cool water and 1 drop organic wool cleanser, and blot dry.

Critical Red Warning

Red clay mud contains mineral iron oxides that act as active dyes. Wet rubbing red clay will permanently stain wool fibers red-yellow. Keep it dry until crusted.

Food Spills (Sauces & Grease)

High Risk Organic Fatty Acid
1
Scrape Solids

Use a dull table knife or spoon to lift paste and thick sauce off the surface. Lift vertically; never slide the spoon, as sliding spreads grease onto adjacent fibers.

2
Mild Dish Soap sponge (Diluted)

Mix 1 drop of gentle clear organic dishwashing liquid with 1 cup of cool water. Dampen a microfiber towel and blot the greasy area. The soap molecules bind and suspend grease.

3
Rinse & Blot Dry

Blot with clean distilled water to remove soap residue. Press firmly with dry towels to draw out all moisture.

Critical Red Warning

Avoid hot water and heat guns. Heat cooks protein stains (like eggs, meat juices) into the wool fibers, turning them into insoluble compounds that cannot be washed out.

Deep Grease Extraction

Tomato paste, oil, and curry spices contain intense natural yellow/red pigments that bind easily. Professional solvent washes are required to extract set grease safely.

Water Damage & Floods

Extreme Risk Structural Moisture
1
Extract Standing Water

If flooded, use a clean wet/dry shop vacuum on SUCTION ONLY mode to draw standing water out of the pile. Work in the direction of the pile lie.

2
Elevate the Entire Rug

Lift the rug off the floor completely. Prop it over chairs, blocks, or clean beams outdoors. Air circulation underneath is vital; drying only the top creates wood floor mold and rug rot.

3
Run Dehumidifiers & Fans

Maximize indoor air flow with multiple floor fans and a high-power dehumidifier. Fast drying stops mold spores from germinating.

Critical Red Warning

Never lay a wet rug flat on hardwood floors. Moisture will bleed dyes, warp wood boards, and generate black rot spores within 48 hours.
