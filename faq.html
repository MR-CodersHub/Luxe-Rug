Answers for Rug Collectors

Have questions about how we treat, wash, or repair antique textiles? Check our compiled answers below.

For rugs in standard residential areas, we recommend a professional hand-wash every 12 to 18 months. High-traffic entryway rugs might benefit from annual cleaning, whereas rugs in formal, low-traffic rooms can go 2 to 3 years. Regular cleaning removes grit particles that act like sandpaper, cutting deep into base fibers and accelerating wear.

Steam cleaning uses high temperatures and high pressure that can dry out wool's natural lanolin oils, making fibers brittle. It also leaves dirty soap residues behind. Our submersive pool hand-wash flushes clean, cool water and organic detergents completely through the rug's front and back foundation. This lifts embedded soils without breaking fibers or melting delicate silk strands.

We execute a pre-wash color stability test on every single shade of your rug. If we discover unstable or bleeding dye compounds (common in natural vegetable dyes), we apply specialized non-toxic organic dye-stabilizing solutions to set the colors before immersion, guaranteeing your patterns remain crisp and sharp.

A standard artisan wash cycle takes approximately 5 to 7 days, including deep dusting, washing, flat drying, and quality reviews. If your rug requires structural restoration, re-weaving, or edge rebuilding, it can take 14 to 21 days depending on the size of the repair zone and knot density.

We offer complimentary white-glove pickup and return delivery for all rugs exceeding 50 square feet within our metropolitan service range. Our staff will carefully move furniture, roll up the rug, load it into our secure vehicle, and re-lay the rug in your home upon return.
