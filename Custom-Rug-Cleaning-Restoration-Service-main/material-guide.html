Understand Your Rug's Foundation

Different rug materials require highly customized washing cycles. Discover how our master weavers evaluate and protect every distinct pile fiber.

Persian

Persian Rugs

High (100+ Yrs)

Hand-knotted with hand-spun wool and organic root vegetable dyes. Famous for intricate medallion patterns and generational longevity.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Vacuum regularly with suction only (disengage the beater bar to protect knots). Rotate 180° annually to balance sunlight exposure.

Cleaning Recommendation

Never steam clean or use abrasive store detergents. Requires professional submersive organic pool hand-wash every 3 to 5 years.

Weaver's Caution

High moisture causes dry rot in the cotton foundation. Spills must be blotted dry instantly.

Oriental

Oriental Rugs

High (80+ Yrs)

Woven across Turkey, India, and China. Includes gorgeous flatweaves (Kilims) and heavy-pile village rugs made from rich local wools.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Sweep with an organic broom or use flat suction. Shake flatweaves outdoors to dislodge grit from flat foundation threads.

Cleaning Recommendation

Needs pH-neutral organic bathing pools. High alkalinity will strip natural lanolin protective oils from the wool pile.

Weaver's Caution

Natural vegetable dyes (indigo, madder) can run if exposed to improper water temperatures.

Pure Silk

Silk Rugs

Medium (Delicate)

Woven with continuous pure silk filaments. Unmatched sheen and extremely high knot density, creating museum-quality tapestries.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Avoid high-traffic areas completely. Hang as wall tapestries or place in bedrooms. Never vacuum with a standard sweeping vacuum.

Cleaning Recommendation

Strictly dry/wet professional hand-cleaning with custom silk-safe solvents. Heat, friction, and moisture will shrink and damage silk fibers.

Weaver's Caution

Liquid stains permanently alter the light reflection pattern of the pile. Act instantly.

Premium Wool

Wool Rugs

Very High

Naturally flame-retardant, elastic, and moisture-repellent. Standard wool area rugs are highly resilient and perfect for dining and family rooms.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Vacuum weekly using low power. Wool is resilient, but sand grit at the base acts like sandpaper, slowly slicing wool fibers over time.

Cleaning Recommendation

Requires wet submersive washing to extract heavy base silt. Organic lanolin-rich cleaners are required to keep fibers soft and elastic.

Weaver's Caution

Subject to moth infestation. Treat with larvae prevention shields if stored for long periods.

Cotton

Cotton Rugs

Medium

Primarily flatweaves and dhurries. Light, breathable, and easily washable, making them a popular choice for kitchens, playrooms, and patios.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Shake out dust or vacuum gently. Cotton lacks the natural stain-repellency of wool, making rapid spill response critical.

Cleaning Recommendation

Avoid harsh laundry powders that turn cotton fibers stiff. Professional hand-washing preserves soft flat structures and fabric weave tightness.

Weaver's Caution

Shrinkage can occur if washed with hot water. Dry flat on tension boards to preserve geometric shapes.

Antique Heirloom

Antique Rugs

Fragile (100+ Yrs)

Fragile collector heirlooms requiring museum-grade protection. Often feature dry structural threads, corroded wool piles, and weakened edges.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Do not vacuum directly. Gently brush surface piles using a soft hand brush or clean with museum-style mesh screen overlays.

Cleaning Recommendation

Requires white-glove artisan restoration hand washing. Handled in specialized air-agitated flat pools to prevent structural stress.

Weaver's Caution

Structural warps are often highly brittle. Rough folding or vacuuming can break dry cotton warps.

Synthetic

Synthetic Rugs

High / Stain Proof

Woven with nylon, polypropylene, or polyester. Highly stain-resistant, cheap, and very durable; designed for standard high-utility homes.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Vacuum regularly with high suction. Stain-resistant, but pile crushing can occur in high traffic paths.

Cleaning Recommendation

Resilient under standard carpet cleansers. However, heavy professional cleaning restores crushed synthetic fibers and flushes trapped grease.

Weaver's Caution

Synthetic fibers are petroleum-based. Exposure to open fireplace heat or intense steam will melt the pile.

Jute & Seagrass

Jute & Seagrass Rugs

Moderate / Eco-Friendly

Sustainable plant-based fibers with a distinct textured weave. Naturally insulating, biodegradable, and perfect for coastal, bohemian, and minimalist interiors.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Avoid excess moisture as plant fibers can absorb water and develop mildew. Blot spills immediately and never steam clean.

Cleaning Recommendation

Professional dry-foam cleaning is preferred. Wet washing can cause significant shrinkage and fiber breakdown in jute and seagrass.

Weaver's Caution

Seagrass is highly absorbent and prone to staining from colored liquids. Use only in low-moisture, low-traffic areas for longevity.

Viscose Silk

Viscose / Bamboo Silk

Low / Delicate Luster

Plant-based regenerated cellulose fiber that mimics the sheen of pure silk at an accessible price point. Often blended into viscose rugs for luminous highlights.

View Care Profile

Care Instructions

Vacuum gently without beater bar. Viscose fibers crush and mat easily under pressure. Rotate regularly to prevent uneven wear patterns.

Cleaning Recommendation

Extremely water-sensitive. Professional solvent-based cleaning only — standard wet washing will cause irreversible shrinkage and fiber swelling.

Weaver's Caution

Viscose is the least durable rug fiber. Any moisture or friction causes permanent pile crush marks and loss of the signature silk-like shimmer.
